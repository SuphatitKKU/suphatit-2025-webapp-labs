My Blog

Building “Tripsback”: My First Big Web App Project

July 11, 2025

Creating Tripsback, a travel web application, was one of the biggest challenges I’ve faced as a developer. I started building it back when I was in Grade 12, at a time when AI tools were not as common or advanced as they are today. That meant I had to search for tutorials, read documents, and learn everything by trial and error.

I decided to use PHP for the backend, even though it's considered old by many people. The reason was simple it had a huge amount of tutorials and support online, which helped me a lot as a beginner. For the database, I chose phpMyAdmin and wrote raw SQL queries to handle user data, trip plans, and rewards.

Time was tight. It was my first time working on a serious project with a real deadline. I worked long hours, often skipping breaks. But during that time, I learned a lot about UI/UX design, creating prototypes, and how to make a website feel smooth and user-friendly.

One of the things I’m most proud of is that I was able to turn Tripsback into a PWA (Progressive Web App) which means it works like a native app on mobile devices. The app allows users to plan their trips, including tourist spots, accommodations, transportation, and even includes mini-games that let users earn points and redeem rewards.

In the end, all the hard work paid off Tripsback won the Gold Medal at the Thailand New Gen Inventors Award when I was in high school.
